Döner kebap

What is it?

A Turkish classic - you cannot complete your trip to Turkey without eating this. Döner meat, usually lamb or turkey, is seasoned, stacked and cooked slowly on a vertical rotisserie. The cook will slice thin layers from the meat ‘cone’ when serving, resulting in a flavorful, crispy yet juicy filling of the bread. Döner kebap also varies in form: there is dürüm (döner with lavaş bread), and iskender kebap (döner on pide bread). The best part? You can have it anywhere: in an upscale restaurant, a fast food bistro, or enjoy a takeaway piece on the go!

Meze

What is it?

Meaning ‘appetizers’, meze is a set of small dishes served at the beginning of multi-course meals. You can also have these individually with alcoholic beverages in meyhane restaurants. The waiter will come with a list, and each dinner member will choose one or more meze. The dishes will then be put in the middle and shared, complemented with toast.

Künefe

What is it?

This sweet treat is all over the streets of Istanbul: you can smell the thin crusty dough filled with unsalted native melting cheese from even far away. The dish is normally served warm, freshly baked, soaked in sugar syrup and topped with pistachio powder. A bite only is capable of waking all of your senses, for the perfect balance it brings about: sweet vs savory flavours, soft vs crusty textures, and even hot vs cold properties if you ask for an ice cream topping.

    • Sema Ceremony

    • Festivals

Grand Bazaar (Kapali Çarsi)

  • Location: Eminounu
  • Opening hours: 9:00 AM - 7:00 PM

Often regarded as one of the oldest shopping malls of the world, the Grand Bazaar is arguably the most popular landmarks in Istanbul, and in 2014 it was crowned as the world’s most-visited tourist attraction with 91,250,000 annual visitors. Within this spacious area with several streets and shops, tourists can enjoy a wide range of products: from glittering jewels, ancient antiques to delicately sewn blankets and much more.

Hagia Sophia (Aya Sofya)

  • Location: Ayasofya Square, Sultanahmet
  • Opening hours: 12:00 AM - 11:59 PM

The heart of Istanbul, Aya Sofya, is definitely a go-to sight for every city visitor. Initially a Byzantine Emperor Justinian’s church, then converted into an Ottoman’s mosque and nowadays a national museum, the building complex has been standing strong for 15 centuries; it, therefore, contains several cultural assets of different historical eras. Tourists can also enjoy the abundant greenery and breath-taking city views.

More Info

Galata Tower (Galata Kulesi)

  • Location: Buyuk Hendek Cad. 2
  • Opening hours: 9:00 AM - 8:30 PM

Located in the quarter of the same name, the Galata Tower boasts its grandeur from even far away thanks to its height of just under 220 feet, dominating the city skyline from virtually any direction. Originally built for defensive purposes centuries ago, the nine-story building is still holding its ground, enriched in history. Tourists will be surprised by the city landscape view as well as nearby attractions - markets, bars, restaurants.

Topkapi Palace (Topkapi Sarayi)

  • Location: Cankurtaran, 34122 Fatih
  • Opening hours: 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM

This magnificent palace, built on the coast of Bosphorus by Mehmet the Conqueror in the 15th century, was the home of the Ottoman rulers. What is so interesting about the Topkapi Palace is the balanced combination of battlemented walls and towers, with decorated rooms, hand-painted tiles in Islamic bright, colourful art. The palace is gigantic - you will need at least half a day to thoroughly explore the complex.
